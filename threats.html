Types of Cyber Threats

    Cybersecurity threats are actions or events that could harm computer systems, networks, or data. Here are some examples:

Common Cyber Threats

  • Phishing Attacks: Fraudulent messages designed to trick individuals into revealing personal information, such as passwords and banking details.
  • Malware: Malicious software including viruses, trojans, and ransomware that can damage computers or steal sensitive data.
  • Password Attacks: Attempts to guess, steal, or brute-force passwords to access private accounts.
  • Social Engineering: Manipulating users into performing actions or disclosing confidential information.
  • Data Breaches: Unauthorized access to sensitive databases, often exposing personal or academic information.

These threats can result in data loss, financial loss, and damage to personal reputation.

Real-Life Examples

Many students have lost access to email accounts or social media profiles due to weak passwords or phishing attacks. Schools and universities have also experienced breaches that exposed student data, highlighting the need for strong cybersecurity practices.
